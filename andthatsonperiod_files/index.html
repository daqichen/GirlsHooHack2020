Learn About Your Body!

Hover on the body to learn about different body parts, what they do, and how they change during puberty. Click on the box if you want to learn more and view the source of the text!

During puberty, the brain undergoes many changes. The hormones estrogen and progesterone increase in production which causes more growth of body hair, moodiness, and development of breasts. Puberty and the reproductive system are controlled by the hormones of the Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Gonadal (HPG) axis. The hypothalamus releases Gonadotropin Releasing Hormone (GnRH) in a pulsatile manner, which stimulates the release of Follicle Stimulating Hormone (FSH) and Luteinizing Hormone (LH) from the anterior pituitary gland.
Acne is a skin condition that shows up as different types of bumps. They include whiteheads, blackheads, red bumps (pimples), and bumps that are filled with pus (called pustules). What causes these annoying bumps? Well, your skin is covered with tiny holes called hair follicles, or pores. Pores contain sebaceous (say: suh-BAY-shus) glands (also called oil glands) that make sebum (say: SEE-bum), an oil that moistens your hair and skin.
As your breasts first start to grow, they can be very tender and sore. They may also itch as your skin gets stretched. Buying a first bra can help protect new breast growth and minimize pain. If the breasts grow rapidly, stretch marks may occur in the skin. These will fade over time. The breasts will continue to grow as the girl’s body fat increases during puberty. They become rounder and fuller. The areola (the area around the nipple) may get darker and larger and the nipple may become erect, or stick out. It’s common for one breast to develop faster than the other. Over time it should even out, however many adult women find their breasts differ very slightly in size. This is completely normal.
The onset of puberty is marked by an increase in the dimensions of the uterus and in endometrial thickness, but also by a change in the shape of the uterus from a tubular to a pear shaped organ. Puberty also marks the beginning of a girl's period which you can read more about in the "About Your Period" section.
Puberty marks the time when hair starts to thicken or grow in areas where no hair grew before. Girls can expect hair to thicken on the legs and grow in the armpits and genital area. The hair doesn’t grow and thicken all at once but in stages that vary from person to person.

Find a Big Sister/Little Sister!

Enter your information below and specify whether you're looking to be a big or little sister. We'll pair you up with someone. The Big Sister/Little Sister program is a great way for younger girls to have someone closer to their age to talk to about all the of the changes that occur both physically and mentally that occur during this period in their lives. As a Big Sister, you can help younger girls to be confident in themselves and help to clear up any confusion that they may have. If you sign up to be a Big Sister, we will reach out, and you will be put through an interview process in order to ensure that you are Big Sister material!
